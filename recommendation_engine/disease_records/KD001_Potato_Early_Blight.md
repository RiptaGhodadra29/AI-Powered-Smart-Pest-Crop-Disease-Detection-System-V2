# Disease Record

## Disease ID
KD001

## Disease Name
Potato Early Blight

## Crop Name
Potato

## Scientific Name
Alternaria solani

## Disease Type
Fungal

## Description
Potato Early Blight is a fungal disease that affects potato leaves, stems, and tubers. It causes dark brown spots with concentric rings and can significantly reduce crop yield if not managed properly.

## Symptoms
- Dark brown circular spots on leaves
- Concentric ring pattern inside spots
- Yellowing of surrounding leaf tissue
- Premature leaf drop
- Reduced plant vigor

## Affected Parts
- Leaves
- Stem
- Tuber

## Cause
Infection by the fungus Alternaria solani.

## Favorable Conditions
- Warm temperatures (24–29°C)
- High humidity
- Frequent rainfall
- Wet foliage

## Severity Level
High

## Yield Impact
20%–50% yield reduction under severe infection.

## Treatment
Use recommended fungicides and remove infected plant debris.

## Organic Treatment
- Neem oil spray
- Compost tea application
- Removal of infected leaves

## Chemical Treatment
- Mancozeb
- Chlorothalonil

## Application Instructions
Apply fungicide according to manufacturer recommendations every 7–10 days during disease-conducive conditions.

## Preventive Measures
- Crop rotation
- Disease-free seed tubers
- Proper field sanitation
- Adequate plant spacing

## Monitoring Actions
- Inspect leaves weekly
- Monitor lower leaves for early symptoms
- Remove infected plant material promptly
